# Privacy Policy — ScreenMirror

_Last updated: 2026-06-29_

ScreenMirror ("the app") lets you mirror one Android device's screen to another
over your local WiFi network. This policy explains what the app does and does not
collect.

## Screen capture

- The app captures your device's screen **only** after you grant permission via
  the Android system "Start recording" dialog, and **only** while a mirroring or
  self-test session is active.
- Captured video is used solely to encode and transmit the mirror stream to a
  receiver **you choose** on your local network, or (in the self-test) to display
  it on the same device.
- The app does **not** upload, store on our servers, or share your screen content.
  Streaming happens directly between your devices over your local network.

## Information we collect

The app itself does not collect personal information or require an account.

**Advertising (free version).** The app shows ads via Google AdMob. AdMob may
collect and process data such as your device's advertising identifier and app
interactions to serve and measure ads. See Google's policy:
https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads

**Purchases (Pro).** Subscriptions are processed by Google Play Billing. We do not
receive or store your payment details; Google handles payment.

## Permissions

- **Screen capture (MediaProjection):** to mirror your screen, only during a session.
- **Foreground service:** to keep capture running reliably and show an ongoing
  "capturing" notification.
- **Network / WiFi:** to stream between devices and to discover receivers on your
  local network.
- **Notifications:** to display the capture status notification.

## Data sharing

We do not sell your data. Ad and payment data are handled by Google under their
respective policies.

## Children

The app is not directed at children under 13.
